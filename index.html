About Patrick

Patrick, a Grant Tower resident, is running for the 2026 APTCA Board of Directors not because he has a grand vision of how to run this community – but because he wants to ensure that residents have a voice in the room where decisions are being made. He believes in honesty, transparency, financial responsibility and in reviewing data and options before making decisions.

By day, he works in software on the aviation side of the industry where he helps to build consensus and compromises in multi-stakeholder situations. In his spare time, he volunteers with a local historical association nonprofit where he serves on their board of directors and recently led a successful effort to rewrite their bylaws for cleaner governance and community inclusion. As a father and husband — heavily outnumbered in his own household — he's a world leader in picking battles, playing the fall-guy, and the judicious use of soft power.

On the Issues

🗣️

Open Decision-Making

I believe residents deserve to understand how decisions that affect their home and finances are being made — not just what was decided, but why. I'll advocate for giving owners a meaningful chance to ask questions, share input, and understand the options before major decisions are finalized.

🏗️

Responsible Stewardship

A well-maintained building protects everyone's investment — but every dollar spent comes from residents' pockets. I'll push for transparent planning that weighs long-term building needs against the real financial impact on the people who live here.

In the Community

Patrick has been an active member of the American Plaza community since day one:

  • Assisted with community surveys and conducted safety demonstrations
  • Serves on the exercise committee
  • Active in the resident assistance program — helping neighbors after minor disasters, assisting with moves, checking on house plants, walking dogs, and generally making himself useful
  • Managed a GoFundMe campaign to support APTCA neighbors after a personal tragedy
  • Serves on the board of directors of a local historical association nonprofit, where he recently led an effort to rewrite the bylaws to align with how the organization actually functions and to give formal avenues for the community to participate in the governance of the club

Guiding Principles

🌳

Long-Term Thinking

I want to plant trees — whether or not I ever get to lie in their shade. Decisions should build for the future, not harvest for the short term.

🤝

Governance as Service

A board seat is a way to give back to the community, not a way to push a personal agenda. Leadership means serving the people who trusted you with it.

🔍

Trust Through Transparency

Good governance starts with open communication. Residents deserve to know what's happening, why decisions are being made, and how their money is being spent.

💰

Financial Responsibility

Every dollar the association spends belongs to the community. That calls for careful stewardship, not carelessness.

🌉

Finding Common Ground

Diverse opinions make us stronger — when we listen to each other. I have a gift for consensus building, finding middle-ground solutions, and leaving egos at the door.

⬆️

Leaving Things Better

Simple as that. Whatever I touch, I want to leave it better than I found it. That's the standard.
